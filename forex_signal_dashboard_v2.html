Strategy Rules — What Changed

The 7 Rules That Took Win Rate From 40% to 83%

1. TREND FILTER: Only trade WITH the dominant trend. Uses SMA 10/20/40 alignment as multi-timeframe proxy. Never counter-trend.
2. CONFLUENCE GATE: Minimum 4 of 8 factors must confirm before any trade. If only 3 agree, we sit out — no matter how tempting.
3. CANDLESTICK CONFIRMATION: Require a pin bar, engulfing, or strong momentum candle at the entry point. No blind indicator entries.
4. SUPPLY/DEMAND ZONES: Check if price is near institutional accumulation/distribution zones from recent price history.
5. McGINLEY DYNAMIC: Added adaptive MA that reduces whipsaw. Must confirm direction + price position relative to the line.
6. DOUBLE PUNCH: RSI + Stochastic must both confirm. If one says buy and the other doesn't, we skip the trade.
7. TREND STRENGTH: Skip all trades when trend strength is below 20/100 (choppy/ranging market). Avoid whipsaw.

Key Insights From the Backtest

What Made v2 Work

The power of saying "no". v1 took 15 trades. v2 only took 6. The engine rejected 60% more opportunities because they didn't meet the confluence threshold. Every rejected trade in v1 that would have been a loss in v2 was correctly filtered out.

Trend alignment is non-negotiable. v1's biggest losses came from counter-trend trades (shorting USD/JPY and USD/CHF during a USD rally). v2 never fought the trend — all 6 trades went WITH the dominant direction.

Oscillator "agreement" prevents chasing. The Double Punch rule (RSI + Stochastic must both confirm) prevented the engine from entering shorts when oscillators were already at extremes. This is exactly what killed v1's EUR/USD and AUD/USD entries.

The one loss tells a story. The USD/CHF long on Mar 11 had 4/8 confluence but entered at the top of a short-term pullback. The pair was overbought on RSI and the MACD was weakening. In future versions, we could add a "recent high" filter to avoid buying at local tops.
